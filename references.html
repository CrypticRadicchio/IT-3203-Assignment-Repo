References

Below you can find the resources used for this website's content. For more information, check out the links.

[1] L. Steelman, “What Is a Web Browser? Web Browser Definition,” wix-encyclopedia, Jul. 28, 2024. https://www.wix.com/encyclopedia/definition/web-browser (accessed Oct. 03, 2025).

This is a good resource to learn the basics about what a web browser is and what it does. This website also has plenty of other information about web design.

[2] Mozilla, “History of the Mozilla Project,” Mozilla, 2013. https://www.mozilla.org/en-US/about/history/

Here is a succinct summary of the history of Firefox and how the Mozilla project began. It also links to many other pages with more information about Firefox and the Mozilla company.

[3] T. Berners-Lee, “Tim Berners-Lee: WorldWideWeb, the first Web client,” www.w3.org. https://www.w3.org/People/Berners-Lee/WorldWideWeb.html>

Notes written by Tim Berners-Lee regarding how he developed WorldWideWeb. He goes into detail about how the browser allowed both browsing and developing.

[4] G. Gromov, “Roads and Crossroads of Internet History,” Netvalley.com, 2012. https://www.netvalley.com/cgi-bin/intval/net_history.pl?chapter=4 (accessed Dec. 08, 2025).

This is only chapter 4 of this online textbook, but the entire book is an excellent discussion of the history of the internet in the 1990s. It also discusses the first browser war and provides statistics on internet adoption.

[5] J. S. Writer Chronicle Staff, “Microsoft Pulls Prank / Company takes browser war to Netscape’s lawn,” SFGATE, Oct. 02, 1997. https://www.sfgate.com/business/article/Microsoft-Pulls-Prank-Company-takes-browser-war-2803749.php

A short article discussing an absurd situation caused by Microsoft and Netscape's heated competition in 1997.

[6] Pavel Panchekha and C. Harrelson, Web Browser Engineering. Oxford University Press, 2024. doi: https://doi.org/10.1093/9780198913887.001.0001.

An excellent book discussing in detail how web browsers are made and what they do. It also discusses the history of the web.

[7] J. Hoffmann, “The Web’s Timeline,” The History of the Web, 2017. https://thehistoryoftheweb.com/timeline/

An interesting website with a good visual timeline of various significant events in the history of the internet.

[8] “Americans Going Online…Explosive Growth, Uncertain Destinations,” Pew Research Center. https://www.pewresearch.org/politics/1995/10/16/americans-going-online-explosive-growth-uncertain-destinations/ (accessed Sep. 14, 2025).

An excellent article written contemporary to the rise of the web in the mid-1990s that discusses the then-current statistics on technology usage.

[9] I. Netto, “Part 1: Internet browsers on mobile phones, a brief history,” Eyeo, Jul. 20, 2022. https://resources.eyeo.com/part-1-history-internet-browsers-mobile (accessed Sep. 14, 2025).

A good article that explains the history of mobile browser development and the problems developers had to overcome.

[10] Imed Bouchrika, “Mobile vs Desktop Usage Statistics for 2025,” Research.com, Feb. 24, 2021. https://research.com/software/guides/mobile-vs-desktop-usage

A great resource discussing the difference in user experience between desktop and mobile devices along with the numerous issues that mobile devices introduced.

[11] “Mobile share of U.S. organic search engine visits 2020,” Statista. https://www.statista.com/statistics/297137/mobile-share-of-us-organic-search-engine-visits/

A graph depicting the increase of mobile internet use from 2013 to 2021.

[12] E. Marcotte, “Responsive Web Design,” A List Apart, May 25, 2010. https://alistapart.com/article/responsive-web-design/

An article discussing responsive web design, media queries, and how web pages are designed to be consistent across all screen sizes.

[13] “Web History Primer,” Oxford Brookes University, 2002. https://web.archive.org/web/20100925204436/http://www.w3c.rl.ac.uk/primers/history/origins.htm (accessed Dec. 08, 2025).

A long but well-written book discussing in fine detail the history of the internet, Tim Berners-Lee, HTML, the W3C, and the many challenges faced during the invention of the internet.

[14] B. Goodger, “Mozilla Firefox Product Development Charter,” Mozilla.org, 2004. https://www-archive.mozilla.org/projects/firefox/charter.html

The charter for the Mozilla project, which discusses the goals for Firefox.

[15] “Browser Market Share Worldwide | Statcounter Global Stats,” StatCounter Global Stats, 2025. https://gs.statcounter.com/browser-market-share#monthly-200901-202511 (accessed Dec. 08, 2025).

A graph depicting the changes in market shares from 2009 to 2025 for all of the major browsers, including Chrome, Internet Explorer, and Firefox.

[16] E. Kennedy, “Font Size Guidelines for Responsive Websites (2021 Update),” www.learnui.design, 2021. https://www.learnui.design/blog/mobile-desktop-website-font-size-guidelines.html

An excellent resource discussing the typography guidelines for designing font sizes and styles on both mobile and desktop screens.
